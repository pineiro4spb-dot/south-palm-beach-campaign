Our Platform: Practical Solutions for South Palm Beach

We're committed to transparent governance under Florida's Sunshine Law. Our focus: Stop wasteful spending and prioritize resident needs.

  • Save Town Hall from Demolition: The 2018 Island Designs Inc. proposal detailed adaptive reuse of our 6,690 sq ft Town Hall (built in phases from 1976-1993) for $1.4M ($1.7M in 2025 dollars), including accessibility upgrades and flood resistance. Former Vice-Mayor Patricia Schulman called it “about perfect” with minor changes. Yet, the Council rejected it for a new 12,000 sq ft building costing $7M—doubling the size and cost without public hearings. We've demanded transparency via public records requests (October 27, 2025). If elected, we'll explore FEMA aid under the 50% Rule for post-disaster renovations instead of demolition.
  • Fix Unsafe Sidewalks: Our sidewalks are crumbling and hazardous, violating municipal safety standards. We'll allocate budget for immediate repairs, seeking grants under federal infrastructure laws to avoid tax hikes. No more delays—safety first for our residents.
  • Fiscal Responsibility: End unchecked spending. Hold public hearings on major projects, as required for issues like sewer fees (e.g., 2021 increases). Make the election a referendum on accountability, challenging incumbents Berendes, Culbertson, and Beckett.
